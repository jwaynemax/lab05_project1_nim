Nim Background

The theory behind the game Nim was discovered by professor of mathematics at Harvard, Charles Bouton in 1901; however, the game is said to have origins as old as the 15th century. In China, Nim was commonly referred to as Tsyanshidzi or the "picking stones game." Two common variations of Nim are Normal and Misère. Normal requires that the winner takes the last object; whereas, Misère requires that the winner avoids taking the last object.
